# Donations

Doctrina is free, MIT-licensed, and built with zero telemetry — it will
never monetise your data. If it saves you time, here is how to give
back.

## Ways to support

| Channel | Where |
|---------|-------|
| **GitHub Sponsors** | [github.com/sponsors/GCarin1](https://github.com/sponsors/GCarin1) |
| **Star the repo** | A star on [GitHub](https://github.com/GCarin1/Doctrina) genuinely helps discovery — it is the zero-cost donation. |
| **Spread the word** | Write about your adoption experience; real-world reports are what the [validation protocol](validation.md) needs most. |

> Maintainer note: additional channels (Pix, Buy Me a Coffee, Open
> Collective) are configured in
> [`.github/FUNDING.yml`](https://github.com/GCarin1/Doctrina/blob/main/.github/FUNDING.yml)
> and appear in the GitHub "Sponsor" button once enabled.

## Where the money goes

Doctrina has no infrastructure bill by design — no servers, no
databases, no APIs. Donations fund maintainer time: triaging issues,
reviewing adapter contributions, keeping the EN/PT docs in parity, and
running the empirical validation work that keeps the framework honest.

## A donation that costs nothing

Run the protocol. Adopt Doctrina in a real project, run
`doctrina metrics --save` before and monthly after, and share the
numbers in an issue — positive or negative. Honest data is the most
valuable contribution this project can receive.
